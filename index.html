About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in theoretical condensed matter physics at McMaster University, affiliated with the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics. My research lies at the interface of quantum field theory, many-body physics, and renormalization group methods, with an emphasis on strongly interacting quantum matter.

My work develops analytical frameworks for non-Fermi liquids with hot Fermi surfaces, quantum critical metals, and unconventional superconductivity. More broadly, I am interested in understanding how collective behavior emerges in interacting many-body systems and in identifying universal mathematical structures that govern strongly correlated phases.

Research Highlights

Projective Fixed Points

Developed the concept of projective fixed points for quantum critical metals, demonstrating how RG flows remain universal despite the growth of the Fermi surface. This work reveals a generalized notion of scale invariance beyond conventional fixed points.

Classification of Non-Fermi Liquids

Developed a unified classification of non-Fermi liquids with hot Fermi surfaces according to the interplay between quantum criticality and superconducting fluctuations, identifying distinct universality classes with different low-energy behavior.

Superconductivity in Quantum Critical Metals

Current work focuses on the evolution of pairing interactions and angle-resolved superconducting susceptibilities in non-Fermi liquids, providing analytical descriptions of Cooper pairing beyond conventional Fermi-liquid theory.
