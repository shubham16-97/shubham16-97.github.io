Publications

My research develops analytical theories of strongly interacting quantum matter using quantum field theory and renormalization group methods. Below is a selection of journal publications and preprints.

Journal Articles

Projective Fixed Points for Non-Fermi Liquids: A Case Study of the Ising-Nematic Quantum Critical Metal

Shubham Kukreja, Afshin Besharat, Sung-Sik Lee

Physical Review B 110, 155142 (2024)

Editors' Suggestion

This work introduced the concept of projective fixed points as the organizing structures of renormalization-group flows in quantum critical metals. The paper demonstrated how universal low-energy behavior can persist despite the continuous growth of the Fermi momentum, providing a generalized notion of scale invariance beyond conventional fixed-point theory.

Classification of Non-Fermi Liquids and their Universal Superconducting Fluctuations

Shubham Kukreja, Dawson M. Willerton, Sung-Sik Lee

arXiv:2601.21047 (2026)

This work develops a comprehensive classification of non-Fermi liquids with globally convex hot Fermi surfaces. The framework organizes quantum critical metals according to their projective fixed points and the renormalization of the Fermi momentum, leading to experimentally testable predictions for emergent symmetry, superconducting instabilities, universal scaling behavior, and crossover phenomena.

123-page manuscript

arXiv

Lack of Scale Invariance of the Angle-Resolved Pair Susceptibility as a Diagnostic of Non-Fermi Liquids

Shubham Kukreja, Dawson M. Willerton, Sung-Sik Lee

In preparation

This work demonstrates that metals possessing abundant gapless modes exhibit rational scale invariance rather than conventional continuous scale invariance. The angle-resolved pairing susceptibility provides a direct diagnostic of this new scaling structure and establishes a new perspective on superconducting fluctuations in non-Fermi liquids.
